CSIS DEPARTMENT STATEMENT

Computer Science and Information Systems (CSIS) is an academic department at Saginaw Valley State University (SVSU), within the College of Science, Engineering and Technology (SET). The purpose of the CSIS department is to prepare students for careers which require both computer programming skills and foundational theoretical knowledge of principles of automation. All CSIS degree programs emphasize hands-on learning. Students spend a significant proportion of time applying what they learn.

DEGREE PROGRAMS

The CSIS department offers three degrees.

  1. Bachelor of Science, Computer Information Systems (BS-CIS)
  2. Bachelor of Science, Computer Science (BS-CS)
  3. Master of Science, Computer Science and Information Systems (MS-CSIS)

1. Bachelor of Science, Computer Information Systems (BS-CIS).

The purpose of the BS-CIS degree is to prepare undergraduates for careers which require exceptional skills in both computer programming and business. Both BS-CIS and BS-CS degree programs require students to demonstrate skills in computer programming. The BS-CIS degree program develops additional business skills.

BS-CIS Careers. BS-CIS holders often pursue careers in data-intensive industries, which increasingly seems to apply to all industries, health care, insurance, retail, etc. BS-CIS holders may be more likely to pursue long term careers in business management. However, the BS-CIS degree does not limit a person to a narrow set of career options. The BS-CIS degree provides a strong foundation for many career directions because computer programming and business skills are required for many career positions.


Fig. 2. The BS-CS degree holder often spends his or her early career solving technical problems which may involve complex mathematics.

2. Bachelor of Science, Computer Science (BS-CS).

The purpose of the BS-CS degree is to prepare undergraduates for careers which require exceptional skills in both computer programming and mathematics. Both BS-CS and BS-CIS degree programs require students to demonstrate skills in computer programming. The BS-CS degree program develops additional mathematics skills.

BS-CS Careers. BS-CS holders often pursue careers in manufacturing and other industries which utilize advanced mathematics. BS-CS holders may be more likely to pursue long term careers in technology leadership, academic research, or corporate research and development (R&D). However, the BS-CS degree does not limit a person to a narrow set of career options. The BS-CS degree provides a strong foundation for many career directions because computer programming and mathematics skills are required for many career positions.


Fig. 3. The MS-CSIS degree holder often spends his or her early career researching advanced technologies, presenting options to nontechnical management, and implementing solutions by coding computer programs.

3. Master of Science, Computer Science and Information Systems (MS-CSIS).

The purpose of the MS-CSIS degree is to prepare postgraduates for careers which require a balance between computer programming skills, theoretical knowledge of computer science principles, presentation/writing skills, and familiarity with advanced computing technologies.
